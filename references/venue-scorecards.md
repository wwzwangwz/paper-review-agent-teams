# Venue Scorecards

Use this reference only after the target venue or journal is known, or when the user asks for scoring.

## CVPR / ICCV / ECCV

Prioritize novelty, technical soundness, empirical strength, clarity, and significance to the computer vision community.

Suggested score:
- 5 Strong accept: clear novelty, rigorous experiments, strong writing, low risk.
- 4 Accept: solid contribution with minor weaknesses.
- 3 Borderline: promising but with notable novelty, evidence, or clarity concerns.
- 2 Reject: important weaknesses in novelty, soundness, experiments, or writing.
- 1 Strong reject: flawed, unsupported, or not ready.

## AAAI

Prioritize technical contribution, broader AI relevance, clarity, reproducibility, and evidence. For anonymous submissions, omit acknowledgments and identifying funding information.

Suggested score:
- 8-10 strong accept range.
- 6-7 accept / weak accept range.
- 5 borderline.
- 3-4 weak reject.
- 1-2 reject.

## NeurIPS / ICLR

Prioritize conceptual contribution, empirical rigor, reproducibility, limitations, and clarity. ICLR-style reviews should include confidence.

Suggested score:
- 8-10 strong accept.
- 6-7 accept.
- 5 borderline.
- 3-4 reject.
- 1-2 strong reject.

## ACL / EMNLP

Prioritize clear NLP relevance, empirical methodology, linguistic/task framing, reproducibility, limitations, and ethical considerations.

Suggested score:
- 5 strong accept.
- 4 accept.
- 3 borderline.
- 2 reject.
- 1 strong reject.

## Journal Review

Prioritize completeness, methodological transparency, related work coverage, limitations, and durable significance. Use recommendations such as accept, minor revision, major revision, or reject.
